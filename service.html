Eagle Scout Project

As a member of Boy Scouts of America, I dedicated hundreds of hours of service in order to advance to the rank of Eagle Scout. This included planning and executing my Eagle Project which took over 2 years of planning and approvals. On the day of execution, I lead more than 30 volunteers to complete the project over the course of over 200 man hours. The site of the project was a local elementary school. We built several components that the kids would go on to use as an outdoor garden classroom in order to learn about the growth of plants as well as other related subjects. In total we moved 4+ cubic yards of soil and mulch for the path and garden boxes, constructed seven 8ftx3ft planter boxes, made one teepee for tomato growth, secured one bench to the ground with concrete, and dug out the space for an 80 ft path. During this project, not only did I give back to my community, but I also learned valuable lessons in both leadership and project management.

Specifications

  • 7x Planter Boxes (8ft x 3ft)

  • 80ft mulch path

  • 12x paver stones

  • 1x Tomato Teepee

  • 1x Bench

  • 30+ volunteers

  • 200+ man hours

  • 2 years of planning
